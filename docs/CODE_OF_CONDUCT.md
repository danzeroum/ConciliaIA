# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* **Being respectful** - Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
* **Being welcoming** - Being open to newcomers and helping them get started
* **Being constructive** - Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
* **Being collaborative** - Focusing on what is best for the community
* **Being professional** - Maintaining a professional demeanor in all interactions
* **Being inclusive** - Using welcoming and inclusive language
* **Being patient** - Showing patience with people who are learning

### Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* **Harassment** - The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
* **Trolling** - Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
* **Disrespect** - Public or private harassment
* **Privacy violations** - Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
* **Unprofessional conduct** - Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
* **Spam** - Excessive self-promotion or off-topic content
* **Discrimination** - Discrimination based on any protected characteristic

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

* GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
* Discord server
* Email communications
* Social media accounts
* Community events (online and offline)
* Any other spaces where you represent the BuildToValue community

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

**conduct@buildtovalue.com**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

**What to include in a report:**
* Your contact information
* Names (real, nicknames, or pseudonyms) of any individuals involved
* Description of the incident
* Additional context (screenshots, links, etc.)
* Whether the incident is ongoing

**Confidentiality:**
All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

#### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Enforcement Process

### Investigation

1. **Report Received** - Community leaders acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. **Initial Review** - Review of the complaint and any supporting evidence (within 3 days)
3. **Investigation** - Gather additional information if needed (within 7 days)
4. **Decision** - Determine if a violation occurred and appropriate consequences (within 10 days)
5. **Communication** - Inform all parties of the decision
6. **Appeal** - Allow 7 days for appeals

### Appeal Process

If you disagree with a decision:

1. Email **appeals@buildtovalue.com** within 7 days
2. Explain why you believe the decision was incorrect
3. Provide any new evidence or information
4. Appeals committee will review (within 14 days)
5. Final decision will be communicated

## Community Guidelines

### Be Respectful in Disagreements

Technical disagreements are normal and healthy, but:

- Focus on ideas, not people
- Assume good intentions
- Be open to being wrong
- Learn from mistakes
- Agree to disagree when necessary

### Give Credit

Always give proper credit:

- Cite sources and inspirations
- Acknowledge contributions
- Thank helpers and reviewers
- Share successes with the team

### Help Others

We're all here to learn:

- Answer questions patiently
- Share your knowledge
- Mentor newcomers
- Create learning resources
- Celebrate others' achievements

### Communicate Effectively

Good communication helps everyone:

- Be clear and concise
- Provide context
- Use appropriate channels
- Respond in reasonable time
- Follow up on commitments

### Respect Time and Resources

Everyone's time is valuable:

- Search before asking questions
- Read documentation first
- Provide reproduction steps for bugs
- Test your changes before requesting review
- Be responsive to feedback

## Specific Guidelines

### For Code Reviews

**Reviewers:**

- Be constructive, not critical
- Explain the "why" behind suggestions
- Praise good solutions
- Ask questions instead of making demands
- Focus on code, not the author

**Authors:**

- Accept feedback gracefully
- Ask for clarification when needed
- Explain your reasoning
- Thank reviewers for their time
- Iterate based on feedback

### For Issues and Discussions

- Search for existing issues before creating new ones
- Use clear, descriptive titles
- Provide reproduction steps for bugs
- Be patient waiting for responses
- Update issues with new information
- Close issues when resolved

### For Pull Requests

- Keep PRs focused and reasonably sized
- Write clear descriptions
- Link to related issues
- Respond to feedback promptly
- Mark PRs as draft if work-in-progress
- Rebase and resolve conflicts

### For Chat (Discord/Slack)

- Stay on topic
- Use appropriate channels
- Don't @mention unnecessarily
- Be mindful of notifications
- Use threads for long discussions
- Respect "do not disturb" status

## Diversity and Inclusion

### Language

- Use gender-neutral language
- Avoid idioms that may not translate well
- Be clear and direct
- Define acronyms and jargon
- Consider non-native English speakers

### Accessibility

- Provide alt text for images
- Use clear formatting
- Support screen readers
- Accommodate different abilities
- Ask about accessibility needs

### Global Community

- Remember different timezones
- Accommodate language barriers
- Respect cultural differences
- Avoid region-specific references
- Use inclusive examples

## Security and Privacy

### Reporting Security Issues

DO NOT report security vulnerabilities publicly. Instead:

- Email **security@buildtovalue.com**
- Use encrypted communication if possible
- Allow time for fixes before disclosure
- Follow responsible disclosure practices

### Protecting Privacy

- Never share others' personal information
- Respect GDPR and privacy regulations
- Anonymize data in examples
- Ask before sharing conversations
- Secure sensitive information

## Consequences of Violations

### Minor Violations

**Examples:**

- Single instance of inappropriate language
- Unintentional offensive comment
- Minor off-topic discussion

**Response:**

- Private warning
- Request for public apology (if appropriate)
- Education about community standards

### Moderate Violations

**Examples:**

- Repeated minor violations after warning
- Sustained off-topic behavior
- Disrespectful language toward others

**Response:**

- Temporary ban (1-30 days)
- Required acknowledgment of violation
- Mediation if conflicts with individuals

### Severe Violations

**Examples:**

- Harassment or threatening behavior
- Hate speech or discrimination
- Doxxing or privacy violations
- Spam or malicious activity

**Response:**

- Immediate permanent ban
- Report to appropriate authorities if illegal
- Notification to other projects if relevant
